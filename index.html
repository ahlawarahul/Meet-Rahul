Telecom BSS Modernization

Industry

Telecommunications

Business Context

The organization wanted to modernize the technology of a multitude of its business-supporting technology functions, including Customer Frontend, Billing, Finance, Middleware, Fulfilment, etc. The objective was to save on costs to manage these applications, while supporting faster Go to Market (GTM) for new products and services.

Key Challenge

Ability to meet the business-defined timelines to complete the change. The various engineering teams were setup for small-scale changes but not such a large-scale initiative.

Applied Efforts

Setup and manage a central program office and org structure to drive various aspects of the delivery and management of the overall program and change. This included building and facilitating coordination between the various engineering teams (including third-party vendors), product owners/business process owners, marketing, finance and other key functions to ensure necessary and timely collaboration on requirements, change intake, delivery/release planning, architecture & design, various decisions, risks, dependencies, and issues. The program office also conducted necessary reporting across all levels, providing visibility to the leadership and the individual teams on progress and key risks/issues.

Result

Effective program management led to this key initiative meeting its planned timelines as well as reducing the overall expected technology costs by $20M p.a., and the time to release new products by 33%.

Insurance IT Standardization Strategy

Industry

Healt Insurance

Business Context

The organization had grown through acquisitions, and was looking to rationalize the technology across various units to standardize and streamline the overall tech footprint while also reducing uncessary costs of maintaining multiple stacks across geographies.

Key Challenge

Defining the roadmap for refactoring business applications and releasing change to various markets, considering interdependies with other business functions & supporting applications, user impact, partner impact (e.g. hospitals), operational process impact and overall risk to a business with 12M+ subscribers.

Applied Efforts

This challenge required a real deep-dive into multiple evaluation criteria for each application, gathering various types of data to understand and define the strategy. Looking into 30+ business processes, 20+ systems and 15+ business & technology teams and,
A. At a high level, assessing the strategic risk by gathering business metrics on each application, such as key use cases supported, customer group & market supported, revenue impact, etc.
B. Secondly, defining operational risk, assessing data on aspects such as count of periodic transactions, external (interface) communications, Ops process impact due to change, level of vendor support, and cost to change, etc.
C. Finally, evaluating technical risk, looking at no. of sub-modules per application and rough lines of code, no. of integrated systems, required people skill to change/maintain applications, etc.

Result

Using the 3-layered matrix of information on strategic, operational and technical risk by application/function, key stakeholders were able to decide on a low-risk 'path-to-change'. The modernization program commenced and was eventually completed in phases over a period of 3 years, producing net savings of 10M+ per annum through the consolidation.
